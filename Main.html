About IEG Portal

Mission

"To provide a global licensing, distribution, and compliance system for casual, e-sports, and educational games." IEG exists to register, verify, and certify digital game titles across these three categories, ensuring each game meets international content and safety standards.

Vision

IEG aims to "become the world's trusted standard for safe, ethical, and legal digital gaming". By enforcing consistent rules and using modern technology (QR codes, blockchain), IEG seeks to elevate trust in digital gaming worldwide.

Organizational Structure

IEG is governed by an international Governing Council and specialized divisions:

  • Governing Council: Global representatives who set policies, ethics, and oversee the system.
  • Game Certification Division: Teams of content analysts, cybersecurity auditors, and educational experts who evaluate game submissions for compliance.
  • Compliance & Regional Enforcement: Ensures games align with local laws in each country and liaises with government agencies.
  • IEG Global Store Management: Curates and promotes licensed titles in the official IEG game store and manages distribution.

This structure ensures clear governance and coordination between developers, regulators, and the IEG store.

Standards and Ethics

IEG adheres to strict international standards:

  • Global Guidelines: UNESCO ethical AI and digital content guidelines, ISO/IEC software and cybersecurity standards, WIPO intellectual property treaties, International Esports Federation (IESF) integrity standards, and key data privacy laws (e.g. GDPR, Philippine Data Privacy Act).
  • Content Ethics: All games must respect moral and cultural norms: no hate speech, no sexual or child exploitation, no deceptive gambling mechanics, and inclusion of responsible gaming warnings.
  • Technical Security: Rigorous technical audits ensure games are free of malware, secure in-app purchases, and fair for competitive play.

By enforcing these standards, IEG guarantees licensed games are safe, legal, and appropriate for players worldwide.

Application Process

Developers submit a license application through the portal, including the following materials:

Game build files (installer, APK/IPA package).
Trailer or gameplay video.
Documentation (game description, target audience, monetization model, data handling statement, country-specific declarations).
Developer/company information and intellectual property declarations.
Initial licensing fee payment (US$20) to start the review process.

The portal then evaluates the submission (content review, technical/security audit, educational validation if applicable, and ethical standards check) over 1-4 weeks.

Fee Structure

The standard licensing fee is US$20 per game per year. This covers one year of certification, including compliance monitoring, updates, and seal revalidation. Developers can renew annually at the same rate. Volume discounts apply for large studios, and optional premium services (fast-track review, featured listings, analytics) are available for additional fees.

When applying, developers must declare the game's category and target audience.

Submission Requirements

To apply for a license, developers must complete all required fields and upload supporting documents. Key requirements include:

Game Build: The executable file or installer (desktop or mobile) of the game.
Gameplay Video: A short trailer or gameplay walkthrough.
Documentation: Written description of the game, intended age group, business model, and data/privacy practices.
Legal Declarations: Proof of developer identity, intellectual property rights, and compliance with any country-specific regulations.
Fee Payment: Proof of the $20 licensing fee transaction.

Incomplete submissions are returned for correction. All materials must be in English or accompanied by translations. A Download Guidelines document is available to help applicants prepare their submission.

Membership Overview

IEG Membership provides developers, organizations, and institutions with official access to the International eDigital Gaming (IEG) Portal. Membership allows users to participate in game licensing, compliance monitoring, and global distribution through the IEG ecosystem.

Membership is designed to ensure accountability, transparency, and verified participation in responsible digital gaming.

Who Can Apply for Membership

IEG Membership is open to:

Independent game developers Game development studios E-sports organizations Educational content creators Academic institutions Government-accredited partners

Types of Membership

Developer Membership

For individual developers and studios who wish to license, publish, and distribute games through the IEG Portal.

Includes:
  • Access to the Developer Portal
  • Ability to register and license games
  • QR Code License generation
  • Annual compliance review access
  • Listing in the IEG Official Game Store

Institutional / Organizational Membership

For companies, schools, e-sports bodies, and organizations involved in digital gaming.

Includes:
  • Multiple developer account access
  • Bulk game licensing options
  • Compliance monitoring tools
  • Priority support
  • Eligibility for partnerships and promotions

Government & Regulatory Membership (By Accreditation)

For authorized government agencies and regulatory bodies.

Includes:
  • Access to the Government Dashboard
  • Country-specific compliance reports
  • Violations monitoring
  • Analytics and policy support tools

Upload & Updates License Renewal Analytics & Reports

Through the portal, developers can upload new games and submit updates or patches for existing titles. The upload panel enforces file format rules and collects version notes. Any update is re-evaluated for compliance (especially if content has changed).

Government Dashboard

Compliance Overview

A secure Government Dashboard is provided for authorized regulators and agencies. This restricted-access interface offers a high-level view of overall compliance. It displays the number of licensed games in each category, renewal statistics, and alerts for any content under review or investigation. Regulators can quickly identify trends in game registrations and monitor the health of the gaming ecosystem.

Reports & Violations

The dashboard includes detailed Country-Specific Reports. Governments can view a list of all licensed games distributed in their jurisdiction, along with any reported incidents. A Violations Database logs games found in breach of content rules (e.g. those requiring suspension or re-evaluation). Each entry includes the nature of the violation and actions taken.

Analytics & AI Insights

IEG provides analytics tools (and planned AI insights) to help governments enforce regulations. For example, automated scans of game metadata can flag possible issues (like undisclosed gambling features). Heat maps and charts show compliance levels by region. These insights support data-driven policymaking and enforcement.
